HHNewton is a small high-performance prototype for solving a nonlinear two-phase flow model in porous media.
The target application is supercritical CO$_2$ injection into a water-saturated formation, with a reduced formulation in which temperature is constant, mechanics is frozen, and dissolved CO$_2$ is neglected.

The model consists of two mass balances,
one for the water phase and one for the CO$_2$ phase.
The unknowns are the water pressure $p_w$ and the CO$_2$ pressure $p_c$.
The capillary pressure $s = p_c - p_w$ controls the water saturation $S_w(s)$, the relative permeabilities, and therefore the nonlinear mobilities.

## Numerical core

The current implementation is based on **MOOSE** and uses a fully implicit Newton solve.
The algebraic system has the block structure

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
J_{ww} & J_{wc} \\
J_{cw} & J_{cc}
\end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix}
\delta p_w \\
\delta p_c
\end{bmatrix}
=
-
\begin{bmatrix}
R_w \\
R_c
\end{bmatrix}.
$$

The transient terms are assembled with lumped mass integration, while the flux terms use cell-centered coefficients for density and mobility.
This detail is important: evaluating strongly nonlinear coefficients at quadrature points or at cell averages leads to different front speeds, even on the same mesh with the same time step.

<img src="/assets/images/07hhnewton/hhnewton_discretization_effect.svg" width="720"/>

The cell-centered variant is deliberately close to a finite-volume discretization:
the pressure gradient is still represented by the finite-element basis, but each element uses one nonlinear mobility state.
This makes the MOOSE implementation algebraically comparable to the MATLAB prototype used for verification.

## Damped Newton

The nonlinear solve is intentionally conservative during the first iterations.
The update is ramped so that the Newton direction is not allowed to destroy the capillary-pressure gap too aggressively.
After the initial guarded phase, the damping increases and the solve moves rapidly toward the fully implicit update.

<img src="/assets/images/07hhnewton/hhnewton_newton_history.svg" width="720"/>

The linear systems are currently solved with a direct sparse factorization through **MUMPS**.
For the reduced model this is a useful reference configuration:
it separates nonlinear modeling errors from Krylov/preconditioning choices and gives a robust baseline before moving to scalable iterative solvers.

## HPC diagnostics

The code path is designed for distributed-memory runs:
mesh partitioning, residual assembly, Jacobian assembly, and direct linear algebra are all driven through the MOOSE/libMesh/PETSc stack.
The diagnostic plot below shows the type of strong-scaling check used while developing the prototype.

<img src="/assets/images/07hhnewton/hhnewton_strong_scaling.svg" width="720"/>

The next step is to replace the direct solve baseline with block preconditioning tailored to the two pressure variables.
The natural structure is already visible in the Jacobian:
storage terms form the reaction blocks, diffusion controls the elliptic part, and the off-diagonal terms carry the saturation coupling through $s = p_c - p_w$.

## Why this matters

HHNewton is not meant to be a large monolithic simulator.
It is a controlled numerical laboratory:

- reproduce the MATLAB model exactly;
- expose the role of coefficient placement in nonlinear flow;
- test damping and globalization strategies for fully implicit Newton;
- build an HPC-ready path toward large two-phase simulations.

The useful part of the exercise is that the implementation is small enough to inspect line by line, but close enough to production infrastructure to test the same numerical issues that appear in large CO$_2$ storage simulations.
